# Part 1 — Business Plan (from `business.md`)

## What This Is

OmniBid is a done-for-you proposal operations service for small government contractors. We deliver compliance matrices, bid/no-bid analysis, RFP shreds, and proposal scaffolding under a monthly retainer. The OmniBid app is **private operator tooling** that lets a single operator deliver work that would normally take a 3–5 person proposal team.

We are explicitly **not** a self-serve SaaS, not a $99 impulse-buy product, and not chasing enterprise primes. Those are different businesses with different economics.

## Why The Service Path

Earlier drafts of this plan pursued a $99 self-serve wedge that climbed a value ladder to enterprise SaaS. We've chosen the service path instead because:

1. **Faster first dollar.** A $2,500 audit closes in weeks of personalized outreach. A $99 self-serve product needs months of compounding marketing before CAC is reasonable.
2. **Cleaner $50k/mo math.** Five retainers at $10k = $50k. Versus roughly 500 monthly transactions at a $99 average with ~80% one-time-buyer churn for self-serve.
3. **The app is not yet a moat.** A compliance-matrix parser is table stakes that any funded competitor (GovDash, Procurement Sciences, Sweetspot, DeepRFP) can ship in a sprint. The defensible position is a workflow, a relationship, and a per-client knowledge base — all of which compound through service delivery.
4. **Trust gap closes by working with people, not selling to them.** Solo operator without a GovCon proposal track record can't sell SaaS to skeptical contractors at scale. We can sell to one contractor at a time with a finished free analysis on the table.

Self-serve becomes a year-two question once we have paying retainer clients, signed case studies, and a validated ICP.

## The Ideal Client

Small-to-mid GovCon firms doing $2M–$30M annual revenue, 10–100 employees, already bidding on federal or state work and feeling proposal pain.

Concretely:

- IT services, cybersecurity, staffing, construction, facilities, logistics, training, engineering, professional services
- At least one prior award; actively pursuing 4–20 bids per year
- No full-time proposal manager, OR a proposal manager who is overloaded
- Owner-led decision making (founder, CEO, VP BD, capture manager)
- SDVOSB / VOSB / 8(a) / HUBZone / WOSB set-aside firms — strong narrative fit; veteran founder status is a trust accelerator

Explicitly not for:

- Defense primes with full capture/proposal departments
- "Want to get into government contracting someday" beginners with no prior bids
- Anyone who needs us to handle CUI before we have CMMC/FedRAMP

## Offer Stack

Five-business-day fixed-scope deliverable on one live or recent RFP the client brings us.

Deliverables:

- Section L/M/C breakdown
- Bid/no-bid scorecard with weighted reasoning
- Risk register
- Required-evidence checklist (gaps in past performance, certs, personnel)

- 30-minute walkthrough call
- Written recommendation memo for ongoing proposal ops

The audit is **not the profit center**. It is the converting offer for the retainer. If the client signs a retainer in the next 30 days, the $2,500 credits toward month 1.

### Tier 1: Proposal Ops Retainer — $5,000/month

Monthly engagement for firms actively bidding.

- Up to 2 RFP shreds per month (compliance matrix + risk memo + outline)
- Bid/no-bid analysis on weekly inbound opportunities
- Section L/M/C extraction with source traceability
- Drafted clarifying questions to the contracting officer
- Shared Slack/Teams/email channel

### Tier 2: Capture + Proposal Engine — $10,000/month

The flagship retainer. Five of these = $50k/mo, which is the cleanest path to the target.

Everything in Tier 1, plus:

For firms wanting an embedded system rather than a delivered service.

Monthly retainer to maintain and operate.

## Risks and Blockers

1. **Liability on missed requirements.** A miss can disqualify a bid. Mitigations: human review on every deliverable before send, E&O carve-out in the contract, decision-support positioning rather than legal/compliance guarantee. E&O insurance ($800–$1,500/yr) is required before first paid engagement.
2. **CUI / CMMC.** We can't touch CUI without compliance investment ($250–$500k, 6–12 months). Mitigation: explicit policy that audits and retainers operate on public solicitations and redacted client materials only. Documented in the data handling policy.
3. **Solo bandwidth.** 5 retainers × 2 RFPs/mo = 10 RFPs delivered with QA, plus sales, plus product. This caps at roughly $50–60k MRR before we need a delivery contractor or a proposal consultant partner.
4. **GovCon credibility gap.** Veteran status helps but we haven't written or won proposals at scale. Mitigation: 1–3 free pilots for case studies, plus disciplined reading of FAR Part 15, APMP materials, and 10–15 published winning proposals.
5. **Sales cycle.** GovCon decision-making is slow. Plan on 4–6 months to first paid retainer, not the 4–6 weeks a SaaS would target.
6. **Concentration risk at low client counts.** With 5 retainer clients, losing one is 20% of revenue. Maintain a paid-audit pipeline at all times so a churned retainer is replaceable in 60 days.

## Where We Are (May 2026)

### Codebase reality

The MVP works end-to-end for a single user, single owner, single RFP. Concretely:

**Backend (`convex/`)**

- `schema.ts` — four tables: `proposals`, `rfpDocuments`, `rawExtractions`, `requirements`. Indexes by owner, status, proposal, category. No team / client / knowledge-base tables yet.
- `proposals.ts` — `create`, `list`, `get`, `generateUploadUrl`, plus internal mutations for status / metadata patching. All scoped to `identity.subject` from Clerk.
- `workflows/ingestRfp.ts` — three-step durable workflow with retries: resolve → extract → validate → mark `matrix_ready`.
- `steps/resolveAndAcquire.ts` — SAM.gov v2 search by `noticeId` or `solnum`, downloads `resourceLinks` (PDF/DOCX) into Convex storage. SSRF guards on URL hostname and final redirect URL. Falls back to direct upload if input type is `pdf_upload`.
- `steps/extractRequirements.ts` — single Gemini 3 Flash call per document with a Zod schema producing metadata + formatting + categorized requirements with citations. One call per doc, no chunking.
- `steps/validateAndOrganize.ts` — third pass; needs review for what it actually does (likely dedup + ordering, not confidence scoring).
- `rfpDocuments.ts`, `rawExtractions.ts`, `requirements.ts` — supporting domain modules.

**Frontend (`src/`)**

- `routes/index.tsx` — patriotic landing page positioned for "Parse an RFP" self-serve. Will be replaced with audit-offer landing.
- `routes/_authed/proposals/*` — list, detail, new-proposal flow with categorized requirements view.
- `routes/sign-in.tsx` / `sign-up.tsx` — Clerk integration.
- Auth gated on `_authed.tsx` layout.

**Stack**

TanStack Start (React 19) + Convex + Clerk + Vercel AI SDK + Gemini 3 Flash + Tailwind v4. Bun for tooling.

## What Works Today

- A SAM.gov URL or a direct PDF upload reliably yields a structured compliance matrix with metadata, formatting rules, and categorized requirements with section/page citations
- Gemini 3 Flash extraction quality on standard federal RFPs (FAR Part 15-style solicitations) is good enough to be the foundation of a paid deliverable, with human review on top
- The Convex workflow is durable: retries on each step, status messages surface to the UI, errors are captured in `errorMessage`
- The owner-scoped data model means we can run multiple clients through one operator account without leakage
- The whole pipeline runs in ~$0.50–$2.00 of API cost per RFP

## What's Broken or Thin

- **No exports.** The matrix only lives in our UI. Clients need Excel and PDF deliverables. This is the single biggest gap to first paid audit.
- **Single-pass extraction.** One Gemini call per doc, no confidence scoring, no second-pass validation against the source. A miss disqualifies a bid; we need a review gate.
- **No client/account model.** Schema is owner-scoped (one Clerk user = one workspace). For retainer delivery to multiple clients we need a `clients` table and per-client knowledge libraries.
- **No knowledge base.** Past performance, capability statements, certs, resumes — none of this can live in the app yet.
- **No opportunity monitoring.** SAM.gov fetch is only on-demand from a notice ID; there's no recurring NAICS-filtered pull for retainer clients.
- **Landing page is for the wrong product.** It sells "Parse an RFP" self-serve, not a $2,500 audit.
- **No payment surface.** No Stripe, no invoicing flow, no Convex Stripe component wired up.

## How Outreach Actually Works

The mechanic for getting from $0 to first paid audit. This is the operational core of the next 60 days.

### The free fit-memo loop

Per prospect, ~20 minutes:

1. From the outreach list, pick a target whose NAICS profile matches a currently-active SAM.gov solicitation
2. Drop the SAM.gov URL into the OmniBid app, get the matrix
3. Spend 10 minutes writing a one-page "fit/risk memo" PDF: agency, due date, mandatory requirements summary, top 3 risk flags, fit score (high/medium/low), and one specific competitive insight
4. Send a 4-line email with the PDF attached:
   > Hey [Name] — fellow vet in Louisiana. [Agency] dropped solicitation [#] last week which looks aligned with [Company]'s past work on [prior contract from public records]. I ran it through my analysis tool and pulled together a quick fit/risk memo (attached). No pitch — let me know if it's useful.

### Conversion path

- Email sent → 5–15% reply rate (realistic for warm-targeted, free-deliverable outreach)
- Reply → 30-min discovery call
- Discovery call → "Do you do this every week, or is it still mostly manual?" → audit pitch
- Audit close → ~25% of qualified discovery calls
- Audit delivered → ~30–50% convert to retainer

Math at conservative rates: 200 sends → 20 replies → 6–10 calls → 1–2 paid audits → 1 retainer. That's the realistic month-2-to-month-4 funnel.

3. Targeted cold email with fit-memo asset (the bulk of volume)

- **Subcontractor sourcing — when, who, and on what basis?** Bandwidth caps at ~$50–60k MRR solo. Decision becomes urgent at 3 active retainers (~month 7).
- **Pricing for revisions and add-ons during the audit?** Default: one revision included, additional billed at $200/hr.
- **What does retainer offboarding look like?** Client owns their knowledge base export; we delete remaining data 30 days post-termination. Codify in MSA.
- **Do we ever publish OmniBid as a public product?** Year-2 question. Re-evaluate at month 12 with real-customer data.

## Risks That Could Kill This

In rough order of likelihood:

1. **Outreach doesn't convert.** 200 sends → 0 paid audits would mean ICP, asset, or channel is wrong. Mitigation: day-90 decision point in the business plan. If 0 paid audits at day 90, revisit before continuing.
2. **Audit delivery quality is below expectations.** First-time proposal services delivery, no GovCon writing experience. Mitigation: 1–3 free pilots before charging, plus reading FAR Part 15 + 10 published winning proposals.
3. **Liability event.** Missed requirement disqualifies a client's bid. Mitigation: confidence flagging shipped before first paid delivery, E&O insurance bound, decision-support disclaimer in MSA.
4. **Solo bandwidth crush at 3 retainers.** Mitigation: don't sign client #4 until subcontractor sourced or delivery patterns are encoded enough to scale.
5. **A funded competitor matches our offer.** GovDash or Procurement Sciences could spin up a similar service tier. Mitigation: be small, be specific, be local. The funded players don't want $5–10k retainer accounts; their unit economics are wrong for it.

## Scaling Plan Bottom Line

The MVP is feature-complete enough to deliver paid work; the gaps are exports, confidence scoring, legal/operational setup, and outreach assets. Sprint 0 ships the sample packet and starts LLC paperwork. Sprint 1 sets up outreach infrastructure. Sprint 2 closes the first paid audits. Sprint 3 converts the first retainer.

Day 90 has 1 active retainer = on track. Day 365 has 5+ retainers = $50k MRR target reached. Everything else is execution.

## Schema Summary

Four tables, all owner-scoped via `ownerId = identity.subject` from Clerk:

- **`proposals`** — top-level entity. `status` enum: `created | resolving | extracting | validating | matrix_ready | error`. Holds solicitation metadata, formatting rules, resourceLinks, requirement count, workflowId. Indexed by owner and status.
- **`rfpDocuments`** — individual files per proposal (PDF/DOCX/etc.), each with its own status and Convex storage `fileId`. Indexed by proposal.
- **`rawExtractions`** — debugging/model-comparison: raw Gemini JSON output, token usage, durationMs. Indexed by proposal.
- **`requirements`** — the compliance matrix rows. Category enum: `mandatory | evaluation_factor | formatting | deliverable | certification | personnel | other`. Each has text, `rfpReference` (e.g. "Section L, Page 42, Para 3.2.1"), status, sortOrder.

**Notably missing for retainer delivery:** `clients`, `clientKnowledge`, `opportunities` tables. These are Ring 3 work, triggered by first retainer signed (not pre-built).

## Pipeline

`workflows/ingestRfp.ts` is a 3-step Convex durable workflow with retries:

1. **`resolveAndAcquire`** — SAM.gov v2 API lookup by noticeId or solnum, downloads `resourceLinks` (PDF/DOCX) into Convex storage. SSRF guards on URL hostname and final redirect URL. Falls back to direct upload if `inputType === "pdf_upload"`.
2. **`extractRequirements`** — single Gemini 3 Flash call per document with a Zod schema. Produces solicitation metadata, formatting rules, and categorized requirements with citations. No chunking.
3. **`validateAndOrganize`** — third pass; current behavior likely dedup + ordering (not confidence scoring). Confidence scoring is a planned Ring 2 add.

Status updates surface to the UI in real time via Convex reactivity. Errors are captured in `proposals.errorMessage`.

## Cost Profile

~$0.50–$2.00 per RFP in API costs (Gemini 3 Flash). Order of magnitude cheaper than the prior LlamaParse + Claude design.

## Known Technical Gaps (blocking paid delivery)

1. No Excel export of the compliance matrix (only CSV exists today). Highest-priority Sprint 0 app work.
2. No PDF export of the proposal-detail view.
3. No confidence scoring on requirements — single-pass extraction means we can't safely flag low-confidence items for human review before delivery. Liability concern.
4. No client/account model — schema is operator-owner-scoped, not multi-client.
5. No knowledge base for past performance / resumes / boilerplate.
6. No opportunity monitoring (recurring SAM.gov pulls).
7. Landing page (`src/routes/index.tsx`) sells the wrong product.
8. No payment surface (Stripe not wired up).
