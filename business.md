# OmniBid: Business Plan

## What This Is

OmniBid is a done-for-you proposal operations service for small government contractors. We deliver compliance matrices, bid/no-bid analysis, RFP shreds, and proposal scaffolding under a monthly retainer. The OmniBid app is **private operator tooling** that lets a single operator deliver work that would normally take a 3–5 person proposal team.

We are explicitly **not** a self-serve SaaS, not a $99 impulse-buy product, and not chasing enterprise primes. Those are different businesses with different economics.

---

## Why The Service Path

Earlier drafts of this plan pursued a $99 self-serve wedge that climbed a value ladder to enterprise SaaS. We've chosen the service path instead because:

1. **Faster first dollar.** A $2,500 audit closes in weeks of personalized outreach. A $99 self-serve product needs months of compounding marketing before CAC is reasonable.
2. **Cleaner $50k/mo math.** Five retainers at $10k = $50k. Versus roughly 500 monthly transactions at a $99 average with ~80% one-time-buyer churn for self-serve.
3. **The app is not yet a moat.** A compliance-matrix parser is table stakes that any funded competitor (GovDash, Procurement Sciences, Sweetspot, DeepRFP) can ship in a sprint. The defensible position is a workflow, a relationship, and a per-client knowledge base — all of which compound through service delivery.
4. **Trust gap closes by working with people, not selling to them.** Solo operator without a GovCon proposal track record can't sell SaaS to skeptical contractors at scale. We can sell to one contractor at a time with a finished free analysis on the table.

Self-serve becomes a year-two question once we have paying retainer clients, signed case studies, and a validated ICP.

## The Ideal Client

Small-to-mid GovCon firms doing $2M–$30M annual revenue, 10–100 employees, already bidding on federal or state work and feeling proposal pain.

Concretely:

- IT services, cybersecurity, staffing, construction, facilities, logistics, training, engineering, professional services
- At least one prior award; actively pursuing 4–20 bids per year
- No full-time proposal manager, OR a proposal manager who is overloaded
- Owner-led decision making (founder, CEO, VP BD, capture manager)
- SDVOSB / VOSB / 8(a) / HUBZone / WOSB set-aside firms — strong narrative fit; veteran founder status is a trust accelerator

Explicitly not for:

- Defense primes with full capture/proposal departments
- "Want to get into government contracting someday" beginners with no prior bids
- Anyone who needs us to handle CUI before we have CMMC/FedRAMP

## Offer Stack

### Tier 0: Bid Readiness Audit — $2,500

Five-business-day fixed-scope deliverable on one live or recent RFP the client brings us.

Deliverables:

- Compliance matrix with source page/section citations (Excel)
- Section L/M/C breakdown
- Bid/no-bid scorecard with weighted reasoning
- Risk register
- Required-evidence checklist (gaps in past performance, certs, personnel)
- Draft proposal outline mapped to evaluation criteria
- 30-minute walkthrough call
- Written recommendation memo for ongoing proposal ops

The audit is **not the profit center**. It is the converting offer for the retainer. If the client signs a retainer in the next 30 days, the $2,500 credits toward month 1.

### Tier 1: Proposal Ops Retainer — $5,000/month

Monthly engagement for firms actively bidding.

- Up to 2 RFP shreds per month (compliance matrix + risk memo + outline)
- Bid/no-bid analysis on weekly inbound opportunities
- Section L/M/C extraction with source traceability
- Drafted clarifying questions to the contracting officer
- Shared Slack/Teams/email channel
- All deliverables human-reviewed before send

### Tier 2: Capture + Proposal Engine — $10,000/month

The flagship retainer. Five of these = $50k/mo, which is the cleanest path to the target.

Everything in Tier 1, plus:

- SAM.gov + state procurement monitoring on the client's NAICS / set-aside profile
- Weekly opportunity-fit memos
- Competitor and incumbent research per pursuit
- Past performance matching against new RFP requirements
- Internal proposal library cleanup and reusable answer bank
- Monthly executive pipeline memo and 60-minute strategy call

### Tier 3: Implementation Sprint — $15k–$25k one-time + $5–10k/month

For firms wanting an embedded system rather than a delivered service.

One-time build:

- Proposal library ingestion into our app
- Past performance database
- Resume / personnel extraction
- Boilerplate library
- Compliance matrix generator scoped to their workflow
- Internal review checkpoints with audit trail

Monthly retainer to maintain and operate.

## The Path to $50k/mo

Target mix: 3 × Tier 2 ($10k) + 4 × Tier 1 ($5k) = $50k MRR. We can also reach it with 5 × Tier 2 alone — same revenue, fewer accounts.

Realistic timeline:

| Month | Milestone | Revenue |
|------:|-----------|---------|
| 0–1   | LLC, E&O, sample packet, landing page, outreach list | $0 |
| 2     | First 1–2 paid audits | $2.5k–$5k |
| 3–4   | First retainer conversion (Tier 1) | $5k–$7.5k MRR |
| 5–6   | 2–3 retainers running | $15k–$25k MRR |
| 9–12  | 5–7 retainers, mix of Tier 1 and Tier 2 | $35k–$50k MRR |
| 12–15 | At or past $50k MRR | $50k+ MRR |

Conservative and assumes modest sales velocity (one closed retainer every 4–6 weeks after month 3). This is not a SaaS scaling curve and shouldn't be benchmarked like one.

## Sales Motion

Cold outreach to GovCon firms generally flops because they're skeptical and over-pitched. We lead with finished work, not pitches.

### Channels (priority order)

1. **APEX Accelerators** (formerly PTACs). Every state has one helping small businesses bid federal contracts. Build relationships with 2–3 counselors; they refer clients regularly.
2. **NaVOBA / Bunker Labs / EBV / regional veteran business networks.** In-person trumps cold email; veteran status is the entry ticket.
3. **SBA VetCert + DSBS targeted lists.** Pull SDVOSB/VOSB lists for LA, TX, MS, AL filtered by NAICS 541512, 541611, 541330, 236220. Yields ~800–1,500 named businesses for outbound.
4. **LinkedIn outreach** to titles: founder, CEO, VP BD, capture manager, proposal manager at firms with prior federal awards.
5. **Proposal consultant partnerships.** White-label arrangement: they keep the client relationship, we deliver the analytical layer. Revenue share or retainer carve-out.
6. **Free pilot bids for case studies.** Plan on 1–3 free engagements before charging, to close the trust gap.

### The Outreach Asset

Don't pitch software. Send a finished free analysis on an active solicitation likely on their radar.

> Hey [Name] — fellow vet in Louisiana. [Agency] just dropped solicitation [#] which looks aligned with [Company]'s past work in [NAICS]. I ran it through my analysis tool and pulled the compliance requirements, deadline risk, and a quick fit/no-bid view. PDF attached. No pitch — happy to walk through it if it's useful.

If they engage, the conversation is: "Do you have a repeatable weekly process for this, or is it still mostly manual?" That's the entry to the $2,500 audit pitch.

## What The App Is Now

OmniBid the app is private operator tooling. It is not customer-facing in this business model. Clients receive PDFs, Excel files, and Word / Google Doc deliverables. They never log in.

### Current capabilities (already shipped)

- SAM.gov URL / solicitation number / PDF upload ingest with SSRF-safe document acquisition
- Native Gemini 3 Flash extraction of metadata, formatting rules, and categorized requirements with source citations
- Durable Convex workflow with retries (resolve → extract → validate)
- Owner-scoped proposal library with status tracking

### Near-term additions, prioritized by delivery impact

1. **Excel export** matching industry-standard Section L/M traceability matrix layout (the deliverable proposal managers actually want)
2. **PDF export** for the bid/no-bid scorecard, risk register, and outline
3. **Confidence scoring** on each extracted requirement, surfacing low-confidence items for human review (liability shield)
4. **Per-client knowledge library** — past performance, resumes, certs, boilerplate (so one operator can serve 5+ clients without context-switching pain)
5. **Opportunity monitoring** — daily SAM.gov pulls filtered by client NAICS profile

### What we do not build

Payments, public sharing, multiplayer editing, public marketing site beyond the audit landing page, self-serve signup, billing portals, team management UI, enterprise SSO. These exist for SaaS businesses. We are not one.

## Risks and Blockers

1. **Liability on missed requirements.** A miss can disqualify a bid. Mitigations: human review on every deliverable before send, E&O carve-out in the contract, decision-support positioning rather than legal/compliance guarantee. E&O insurance ($800–$1,500/yr) is required before first paid engagement.
2. **CUI / CMMC.** We can't touch CUI without compliance investment ($250–$500k, 6–12 months). Mitigation: explicit policy that audits and retainers operate on public solicitations and redacted client materials only. Documented in the data handling policy.
3. **Solo bandwidth.** 5 retainers × 2 RFPs/mo = 10 RFPs delivered with QA, plus sales, plus product. This caps at roughly $50–60k MRR before we need a delivery contractor or a proposal consultant partner.
4. **GovCon credibility gap.** Veteran status helps but we haven't written or won proposals at scale. Mitigation: 1–3 free pilots for case studies, plus disciplined reading of FAR Part 15, APMP materials, and 10–15 published winning proposals.
5. **Sales cycle.** GovCon decision-making is slow. Plan on 4–6 months to first paid retainer, not the 4–6 weeks a SaaS would target.
6. **Concentration risk at low client counts.** With 5 retainer clients, losing one is 20% of revenue. Maintain a paid-audit pipeline at all times so a churned retainer is replaceable in 60 days.

## 90-Day Plan

**Days 1–14 — Foundations.** LLC, E&O, MSA + SOW templates, data policy. Excel export shipped. One sample packet built end-to-end from a public RFP.

**Days 15–30 — Distribution Setup.** Landing page repositioned for the audit offer. Loom walkthrough recorded. First outreach list compiled. APEX Accelerator counselor in Baton Rouge contacted for an intro meeting. First 25 cold outreach emails sent with attached fit-memos.

**Days 31–60 — First Dollars.** Continue outreach (target ~50/week). Goal: 1–2 paid audits closed. Begin first audit delivery. APEX intro meeting completed. First veteran business network event attended in person.

**Days 61–90 — Convert to Retainer.** Deliver paid audits, convert at least one to a Tier 1 or Tier 2 retainer. Begin building per-client knowledge libraries inside the app. Document repeat-delivery patterns to encode into operator tooling.

**Day 90 decision point.** If zero paid audits closed and zero retainers signed, revisit ICP and channel mix before continuing investment. Likely failure modes: wrong NAICS targeting, weak sample packet, outreach asset not differentiated enough.

---

## Bottom Line

OmniBid stops being a product company and becomes a service business with proprietary tooling. The $50k/mo target is reachable in 12–15 months with disciplined sales execution. The app keeps shipping features, but only the ones that make our delivery faster — not features for paying users to consume directly. We revisit the self-serve product question at month 12 with a year of real-customer data instead of speculation.
